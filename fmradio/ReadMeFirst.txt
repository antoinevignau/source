LaCie FM Radio New Desk Accessory		  (c) 2026 by Brutal Deluxe__________________________________________________________________Listen to your favorite FM stations from your Apple IIgs computer! The FM Radio ADB device shipped in 1996 for the Macintosh. We wrote a New Desk Accessory to drive the FM Radio directly from your Apple IIgs.FM Radio hardware setup_____________________________________________1/ Shut down and power off the Apple IIgs before connecting the FM Radio.Important: ignoring this warning may result in severe damage to both the ADB port and the Apple IIgs motherboard.2/ Disconnect the keyboard cable from the back of the Apple IIgs (it is attached to the ADB port).3/ Connect the FM Radio ADB cable to the ADB port of the Apple IIgs and daisy chain the keyboard cable to the ADB cable stub.Important: always connect the FM Radio directly to the Apple IIgs ADB port. Do not connect it to the keyboard or screen.4/ Plug one end of the audio cable into the microphone jack on the FM Radio. Plug the other end of the audio cable to external powered speakers.5/ Spread out the FM Radio built-in antenna and hook it up in a vertical position.6/ You are now ready to start up the Apple IIgs and use the FM Radio. Installation Manual________________________________________________1/ Insert the FM Radio diskette in the Apple IIgs floppy drive.2/ Double-click the "Installer" program.3/ Install the "Desk Acc: LaCie FM Radio" extension script onto your boot disk.The following files are copied onto your startup disk:	- System:Desk.Accs:FMRadioNDA	- System:Fonts:LED.244/ Restart your computer.Main window features________________________________________________	----------------------------------------	|  ---------       |-- (1) --|  (2-3)  |	|  |  (4)  |                           |	|  ---------       |-- (5) --|   (6)   |	|                                      |	|    |--------- (7) ---------|   (8)   |	----------------------------------------ź Use the pop-up menu (1) to switch between pre-programmed stations.ź Use the "-" button (2) to remove the selected pre-programmed station.ź Use the "+" button (3) to add the current station to the list of pre-programmed stations.You can store up to 16 preset stations in the Radio FM.
